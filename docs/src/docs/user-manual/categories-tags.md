# Categories & Tags

Comaney gives you two ways to organise your expenses: **categories** and **tags**. They work differently, and using both together gives you a much clearer picture of your spending.

## Categories: one bucket per expense

Every expense belongs to at most one category. Think of a category as the main label for where money went: *Groceries*, *Housing*, *Transport*, *Entertainment*.

When you add or edit an expense, you pick one category from the dropdown, or leave it blank.

**Good uses for categories:**

- Groceries
- Housing
- Transport
- Healthcare
- Entertainment
- Utilities

Because each expense can only be in one category, your pie chart will always add up to 100% and slices will make sense.

### Managing your categories

1. Click **Categories & Tags** in the navigation.
2. To create a new category, type a name and click **Create**.
3. To rename a category, click the pencil icon next to it.
4. To delete a category, click the bin icon. The expenses that used it are not deleted; they just become uncategorised.

## Tags: flexible extra labels

Tags are more flexible. An expense can have as many tags as you like, or none at all. Tags are great for things that cut across categories.

**Good uses for tags:**

- *Credit card* / *Debit card* / *Cash* (how you paid)
- *Amazon* (where you bought it)
- *Subscriptions* (type of charge)
- *Work* (reimbursable expenses)
- *Partner* (shared costs)

For example, your monthly Amazon order might be in the *Groceries* category and also carry the tags *Amazon* and *Credit card*. Later you can filter by any of those.

### Managing your tags

Same as categories: go to **Categories & Tags**, create, rename, or delete tags there. Deleting a tag removes it from all expenses that had it, but the expenses themselves are not deleted.

## Using both together

A common approach:

- **Categories** answer "what is this money for?": Groceries, Rent, Healthcare.
- **Tags** answer "how or where?": Credit card, Amazon, Recurring, Partner.

This lets you answer questions like "How much did I spend on groceries using the credit card?" by searching for `cat=Groceries tag="credit card"` in the expense list.

## Filtering by category or tag

In the search bar:

- `cat=Groceries` finds expenses in any category whose name contains "Groceries".
- `tag=amazon` finds expenses with any tag containing "amazon".
- `cat=none` finds expenses with no category.
- `tag=none` finds expenses with no tags at all.

See [Search & Filters](dashboard/query-language.md) for the full reference.

## Catalog Partnerships

If you split expenses regularly with another person, you can link your catalogs so that any tag or category either of you creates, renames, or deletes is automatically reflected in the other person's account too.

When you are in a Catalog Partnership, a banner at the top of this page reminds you, and the delete confirmation tells you that the change will affect all your partners.

See [Catalog Partnerships](catalog-partnerships.md) for the full guide.
