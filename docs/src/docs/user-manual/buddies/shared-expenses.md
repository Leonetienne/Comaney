# Shared Expenses

Any expense can be turned into a shared expense. You decide who is involved, how the cost is split, and who actually paid.

## Logging a shared expense

1. Create a new expense as usual (**Expenses** > **New expense**).
2. Tick **This is a buddy payment**.
3. A sharing section appears. Fill in the three parts:

**Who took part** — Select who is involved with this expense. You can select either a personal buddy or a buddy group. You can exclude group members.

**How much each person owes** — use the sliders to modify percentages directly or click **Equal shares** to split the cost evenly among everyone ticked.

**Who paid upfront** — by default this is you. If someone else paid the full amount (for example a friend covered the whole dinner), select their name from the list.

4. Fill in the rest of the expense as normal and click **Save**.

!!! note "When someone else paid upfront"
    If you select another person as the one who paid, the expense will not appear in your expense list, because the money did not come out of your own pocket.
    
    - If the other person is an offline buddy, the expense will appear on the **Buddy Expenses** page instead.
    - If the other person is a real user, the expense will be created for them, in their account. They will have to approve it. The expense will also appear on the **Buddy Expenses** page. 

## Where shared expenses appear

- **Buddy Expenses** shows all shared expenses you are involved in, whether you created them or not.
- **Buddy Expenses** shows the current balance (who owes who) across all shared expenses.
- **Group pages** show the breakdown for that group specifically.

## Approving or rejecting an expense someone created for you

If a connected buddy creates a shared expense and marks you as the one who paid upfront,
you need to confirm it before it counts toward the balance and your personal expense tracking.

You will see it in the **"Did you pay for this?"** section on the **Buddy Expenses** page.

- Click **Approve** if it is correct. The expense moves into your regular expense list and the balance updates.
- Click **Reject** if it is wrong. The expense is permanently deleted, and the person who created it receives an email notification.

!!! tip
    You will also receive an email notification when someone creates a shared expense on your behalf, so you do not have to remember to check.

## Editing a shared expense

Open the expense from your expense list or from the **Buddy Expenses** page and click **Edit**.
The sharing section will be pre-filled with the current settings.
You can adjust the participants, percentages, and who paid, then save.
You can only edit your own expenses or the ones of your offline buddies.
Within a group, only the *group admin* can edit offline buddy expenses.

## Removing an expense from a group

On a group's detail page, each expense in the breakdown has two possible actions (depending on your permissions):

| Action | What it does                                                                                                                      |
|---|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Delete** | Permanently deletes the expense for all group members and their personal account. Only the expense owner may perform this action. |
| **Unlink** | Removes the expense from the group while keeping it in the owner's own expense list as a regular, non-shared expense.             |

!!! warning
    Deleting an expense removes it for everyone and cannot be undone. Use **Unlink** if you only want to remove it from the group without losing the record.
    You can only delete your own expenses, not even group admins can delete your expenses.
